# Limitations and Methods Supplement

*Drop-in text for a Limitations / Methods-caveats section. Each item maps to a specific, checkable artifact in this repository rather than a general disclaimer.*

1. **Non-circularity of RQ5.** To avoid RQ5's prescriptive value function being mechanically derived from RQ2's concentration metric (cascade-impact under degree removal) or RQ3's spillover-share metric, RQ5 is defined here on an independent composite of closeness centrality, eigenvector centrality, demand centrality, and utilization centrality — none of which enter RQ2 or RQ3's target variables. Convergence between this independently-defined RQ5 selection and the RQ2/RQ3/RQ4 top-5 lists is tested against a permutation null of random out-of-scope picks (Fig. S5). **Result:** significant overlap with RQ2 (p = 0.001); overlap with RQ3 and RQ4 is directionally consistent but not statistically distinguishable from chance (p = 0.15–0.58) at K = 5. **This item is a necessary but not sufficient de-circularization step** — see item 11 for the quantitative audit of how independent the four RQ5 inputs actually are from the RQ2 metric, and item 12 for how that changes the interpretation of the p = 0.001 result above.

2. **Effective sample size in RQ4.** The augmented dataset contains 95 observations drawn from only 19 independent stations, each measured under 5 K-ETS scenarios — a repeated-measures design, not 95 independent samples. All cross-validation is clustered at the corridor level (leave-one-corridor-out) to respect this dependence structure; the effective independent N is reported alongside every metric.

3. **Hyperparameter selection.** The ridge penalty (α) for the SGC-ridge transfer model is selected by nested leave-one-corridor-out cross-validation over a log-spaced grid (0.1, 0.5, 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100), not fixed a priori. The selected value is α = 0.1 (Fig. S3).

4. **Multiple comparisons.** RQ1 reports bootstrap CIs for the Optimization Coverage Gap under 4 distinct centrality weightings. A Holm–Bonferroni correction (m = 4) is applied to the implied two-sided test against a 50% null; the qualitative conclusion — CIs do not exclude 50% at N = 53 — is unchanged before and after correction (Fig. S1).

5. **Statistical comparator for concentration.** RQ2's Gini coefficients are benchmarked against a permutation null in which the same total structural-gap mass is allocated uniformly at random (Dirichlet(1,...,1)) across the same N out-of-scope stations. Observed concentration exceeds this null at p < 0.001 for all three centrality weightings tested (Fig. S2). Degree-based concentration is the exception: it is statistically indistinguishable from the null (p = 0.872), and this is reported explicitly rather than omitted in favor of the two weightings that do clear the null — see item 13.

6. **Scenario-probability sensitivity.** The K-ETS scenario probabilities used in RQ5's expected-value objective are a modeling assumption. A sensitivity sweep over 200 draws from the probability simplex shows the same top-5 station set is recovered in 100% of draws (Fig. S4) — i.e., the selection is robust to this particular assumption even though the assumption itself is not empirically derived.

7. **LLM-audit verifier validation.** The deterministic verifier underlying the Explainer/Auditor faithfulness pipeline achieves a perfect pass rate on 34 real out-of-scope narratives. To confirm this reflects genuine claim correctness rather than a permissive verifier, a synthetic stress test injects known-incorrect claims (flipped direction / shifted rank) at controlled corruption rates and measures detection sensitivity and specificity (Fig. S6). Faithfulness scores on the real narratives are additionally reported with 95% bootstrap confidence intervals rather than as a bare point estimate (Fig. S7).

8. **Data provenance.** All source CSVs are retrieved from two public GitHub repositories. Exact commit SHAs and SHA-256 checksums of the retrieved bytes are recorded in a data-provenance manifest for reproducibility; these are cited in the manuscript's Data Availability statement.

9. **Small-N disclosure.** The out-of-scope station set has N = 34; several RQ2/RQ5 comparisons therefore carry a small-sample flag. This is stated explicitly wherever N < 30, rather than left implicit. This is also the direct reason the convergence test in item 1/12 has limited statistical power to detect anything short of near-total overlap — a smaller candidate pool makes chance overlap easier to achieve, which the 20,000-draw permutation null accounts for but does not eliminate as a source of imprecision.

10. **Model simplicity as a scoping choice.** The RQ4 transfer model is intentionally linear (SGC-propagated ridge regression). This is what makes exact, non-sampled SHAP attribution possible and is central to the "AI trustworthiness" contribution claimed for this line of work — but it also means the audited "AI extrapolation" step is simpler than a deep model. We treat this as a stated scoping decision (auditability traded against model capacity) rather than an unstated one, and note it as a natural direction for follow-up work with nonlinear transfer models and sampled or kernel SHAP.

11. **Single-topology dependence of the RQ2 concentration finding, and the robustness check applied to it.** A concentration statistic computed on one empirical network is, by construction, vulnerable to the objection that the specific wiring observed — rather than the network's more general structural properties — produced the result. This is addressed directly with an eight-realization ensemble of degree-preserving double-edge-swap rewirings of the empirical topology (independently seeded: 42, 137, 256, 512, 1024, 2048, 4096, 8192), each preserving the exact empirical degree sequence while randomizing specific station-to-station connections. Betweenness-weighted cascade-impact Gini is recomputed on every realization and benchmarked against a realization-specific permutation null (2,000 draws). **Result:** Gini ranges from 0.614 to 0.700 across the ensemble (mean = 0.673, SD = 0.032), and every realization clears its own null at p < 0.001; mean top-5 station-identity overlap with the empirical topology is 3.75/5 (range 2/5–4/5), with the two highest-impact stations recovered in all eight realizations. This is read as evidence that the concentration is a structural consequence of the degree distribution rather than an artifact of one edge list — **but it is not a substitute for independent replication on a second national network,** since a rewiring ensemble is still drawn from the same network's own degree sequence. That remains an open item for future work (see repository's future-research notes).

12. **Assumed vs. measured feature independence between RQ2 and RQ5, and its effect on the convergence claim.** Item 1 above states that RQ5 is built from features that are not literally the RQ2 metric; this item quantifies how independent those features actually are, since non-identical naming does not guarantee low correlation on a shared topology. Pearson/Spearman correlations and r² (shared variance) are computed between betweenness centrality (the RQ2 metric) and each of RQ5's four inputs, across all 53 stations: closeness (r² = 27.0%), eigenvector (r² = 37.8%), demand (r² = 76.6%), utilization (r² = 84.5%) — all significant at p < 0.001 given N = 53, which is expected at this sample size and not itself the relevant signal; the r² magnitude is. Closeness and eigenvector centrality are judged moderately distinct and contribute genuine independent evidence to the convergence test; demand and utilization centrality are strongly collinear with betweenness and function, in practice, as partial restatements of the RQ2 signal under different names. **Consequence for item 1's p = 0.001 convergence result:** the reported 4/5 top-5 overlap between RQ2 and RQ5 should be read as roughly half genuine convergent validity (from the closeness/eigenvector components) and half a product of shared topological origin (from the demand/utilization components), not as evidence that two fully independent procedures reached the same conclusion. This is stated as a two-tier finding rather than compressed into the single p-value.

13. **Weighting-dependence of the concentration conclusion.** Degree-based concentration is statistically indistinguishable from a random allocation of the same total mass (Gini = 0.429, p = 0.872 against the permutation null), while betweenness- and cascade-impact-based concentration both clear the null decisively (p < 0.001). This means the choice of centrality weighting used to justify a scope-expansion decision materially changes the conclusion one would draw. Rather than presenting only the weightings that support the concentration narrative, all three tested weightings — including the null result for degree — are reported side by side (Table B1 / Fig. S2), and any operational use of this framework should state explicitly which weighting was used and why.
